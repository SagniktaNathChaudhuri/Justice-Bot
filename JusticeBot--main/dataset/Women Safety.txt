Women Safety

1. The Indian Penal Code (IPC), 1860
Section 354: Deals with assault or criminal force against a woman with the intent to outrage her modesty. It includes provisions against actions like stalking, voyeurism, and disrobing.
Section 375: Defines the offense of rape, providing a comprehensive definition that includes various forms of non-consensual sexual intercourse. This section also prescribes stringent punishments for rape.
Section 498A: Addresses cruelty by a husband or his relatives towards a woman, which includes any willful conduct likely to drive the woman to suicide or cause grave injury.
Section 509: Punishes acts intended to insult the modesty of a woman, such as making any sound or gesture or exhibiting any object to intrude upon a woman’s privacy.
2. The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (PWDVA)
This act provides a comprehensive framework for protecting women from all forms of domestic violence, including physical, emotional, sexual, and economic abuse. It allows women to seek protection orders, residence orders, monetary relief, custody orders, and compensation orders.

Protection Orders: Aimed at preventing further acts of domestic violence.
Residence Orders: Ensures that women are not forcefully evicted from their homes.
Monetary Relief: Provides financial compensation to cover losses and damages suffered by the victim.
3. The Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013
This act provides protection against sexual harassment of women at the workplace and aims to create a safe and secure working environment for women. It mandates the establishment of an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) in every organization with more than ten employees.

Definition of Sexual Harassment: Includes any unwelcome act or behavior, such as physical contact, a demand or request for sexual favors, making sexually colored remarks, showing pornography, or any other unwelcome physical, verbal, or non-verbal conduct of a sexual nature.
Employer's Duties: Employers are required to take appropriate steps to prevent sexual harassment, provide assistance to the woman if she wishes to file a police complaint, and ensure a fair and timely inquiry.
4. The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961
This act prohibits the giving or taking of dowry in India. It also makes it illegal to demand dowry directly or indirectly. Violation of this act is punishable with imprisonment and a fine.

Definition of Dowry: Any property or valuable security given or agreed to be given either directly or indirectly by one party to a marriage to the other party.
Penalties: Imprisonment ranging from a minimum of five years and a fine of up to ₹15,000 or the value of the dowry, whichever is higher.
5. The Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956 (ITPA)
This act aims to combat human trafficking and commercial sexual exploitation. It penalizes the trafficking of women and girls for the purpose of prostitution and provides for stringent punishment for those involved in trafficking activities.

Punishments for Trafficking: Includes penalties for procuring, inducing, or taking a person for the sake of prostitution.
Rehabilitation Provisions: Includes measures for the rescue and rehabilitation of victims of trafficking.
6. The Indecent Representation of Women (Prohibition) Act, 1986
This act prohibits indecent representation of women through advertisements, publications, writings, paintings, or figures, or in any other manner.

Definition of Indecent Representation: The depiction in any manner of the figure of a woman, her form or body, or any part thereof, in a manner that is derogatory, denigrating, or likely to deprave, corrupt, or injure the public morality or morals.
7. The Maternity Benefit Act, 1961
This act regulates the employment of women in certain establishments for a certain period before and after childbirth and provides maternity benefits, such as maternity leave, nursing breaks, and other related benefits.

Maternity Leave: Extended to 26 weeks for women employed in both the organized and unorganized sectors.
Crèche Facility: Establishments with more than 50 employees are required to have a crèche facility.
8. The Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971 (Amended in 2021)
This act allows for the legal termination of pregnancy under specific conditions and safeguards women’s reproductive rights.

Conditions for Abortion: Pregnancy may be terminated up to 20 weeks with the approval of one medical practitioner, and from 20 to 24 weeks with the approval of two medical practitioners, especially in cases involving survivors of rape or incest, or where there is substantial risk to the mother’s health or life.
Privacy: The act mandates the protection of the privacy of women seeking an abortion.
9. The Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013 (Nirbhaya Act)
Enacted following the 2012 Delhi gang rape case, this amendment made significant changes to the IPC, the Indian Evidence Act, and the Code of Criminal Procedure, to provide stricter punishment for crimes against women.

New Offenses Introduced: Includes stalking, acid attacks, and voyeurism as specific crimes with distinct punishments.
Stricter Punishments: Enhanced penalties for rape, including the death penalty for repeat offenders and gang rape cases.
10. The Prohibition of Child Marriage Act, 2006
This act prohibits the marriage of girls under the age of 18 and boys under the age of 21. It aims to prevent child marriages and provides for the protection and rehabilitation of the victims.

Penalties: Includes imprisonment up to two years and a fine of up to ₹1 lakh for those who facilitate or participate in child marriages.
